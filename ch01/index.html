Scones: the most resplendent of snacks

Occassionally maligned and misunderstood; the scone is a quintessentially British classic.

Incredible scones, picture from Wikipedia

Recipe and srving suggestions follow.

Ingredients

  • 500g self-raising flour
  • 1tsp salt
  • 1tsp baking powder
  • 1/4tsp bicarbonate of soda
  • 1/2tsp cream of tartar
  • 150g butter
  • 150g granulated sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 3/4pt milk
  • 1/2tsp vanilla extract

Method

  1. Preheat oven to 220 C (425 F, Gas Mark 7). Line a baking sheet with parchment paper.
  2. In a large bowl, whisk together the flour, salt, baking powder, bicarbonate of soda, and cream of tartar.
  3. Add the butter and use your fingertips or a pastry blender to work it into the flour mixture until it resembles coarse breadcrumbs.
  4. Add the sugar, egg, milk and vanilla extract. Mix until a dough forms.
  5. Turn the dough out onto a floured surface and gently knead it a few times until it comes together.
  6. Pat the dough into a circle that is about 1 inch (2.5 cm) thick.
  7. Use a biscuit cutter or the rim of a glass to cut out the scones. You can also just use a knife to cut the dough into squares or triangles.
  8. Place the scones on the prepared baking sheet, leaving about 1 inch (2.5 cm) of space between each scone.
  9. Bake the scones for 15-20 minutes, or until they are golden brown.
